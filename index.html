What We Do

As professors, we strive tirelessly to shape the minds of tomorrow, mostly by engaging in processes that are as enigmatic as they are labyrinthine.

Endless Committee Meetings: Much of our time is spent in carefully scheduled committee meetings, each with an agenda that spans everything from “suggested font sizes for departmental memos” to the “ongoing exploration of coffee machine reliability.” Consensus is often reached only after prolonged debate on Robert’s Rules of Order, after which the original topic is tabled for “further research.”

Research Proposal Marathons: Obtaining research funding is a delicate art, involving the perfect blend of scientific jargon, optimistic promises of groundbreaking discovery, and strategic use of buzzwords like “interdisciplinary synergy” and “data-driven.” The true mastery lies in submitting this proposal at precisely 11:59 PM, an hour universally accepted as Peak Academic Productivity.

Syllabus Optimization: Crafting the perfect syllabus is an annual event, requiring weeks of careful language adjustments to ensure students understand that “suggested readings” are, in fact, mandatory. This includes footnotes on the footnotes, as well as reminders about “office hours,” which are largely attended by faculty members in search of chocolate.

Peer Review Mysteries: The peer review process remains shrouded in mystery, a scholarly rite of passage that often involves receiving feedback such as “this analysis lacks robustness” or “consider exploring other disciplines.” Scholars cherish the thrill of waiting months to be told to “please resubmit with major revisions”—a phrase that echoes in academia like a rallying cry.

Our History

The history of our chapter dates back to the early days of academia, where theories, discussions, and caffeinated debates were forged in the crucible of collegial discourse.

The Early Years: In 1892, scholars engaged in a heated debate on whether footnotes should be positioned at the bottom of the page or relegated to oblivion (also known as the appendix). This monumental discussion is rumored to have lasted three semesters, resulting in no fewer than 47 footnotes about footnotes.

The Rise of Peer Review: By 1957, our chapter pioneered the concept of “peer review,” where papers are scrutinized by an esteemed group of colleagues. Initially, however, “peer review” was shorthand for “let’s see who can find the most typos,” an art form perfected through fierce rivalry and red ink.

Modern Developments: Fast forward to today, where we’ve embraced technological advancements. A recent victory includes the installation of a coffee machine capable of sustaining the most challenging 8-hour dissertation defenses. Additionally, the chapter’s motto, “Publish or Publ-ishn’t,” now graces our quarterly newsletters.
